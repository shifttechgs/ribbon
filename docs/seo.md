## 1. EXECUTIVE SEO STRATEGY

### The Core Thesis
Cape Town homeowners searching for a plumber fall into two categories:

1. **Emergency intent** (60% of searches): "emergency plumber cape town", "burst pipe", "no hot water now". These people are calling within 30 seconds of clicking. They need to land on a page that answers the phone.

2. **Research intent** (40%): "how much does a geyser cost in Cape Town", "gas geyser vs electric". These people become customers 3–14 days later. They need educational content that builds trust.

The SEO strategy must serve both. Emergency traffic: fast, high-converting landing pages. Research traffic: authoritative, locally-specific blog content.

### Why SEO Will Win Here
- Cape Town plumbing market: ~48 active competitors, but most have weak SEO
- Most competitors rely on word-of-mouth or directory listings, not organic search
- The combination of plumbing + gas keywords on one website creates cross-service authority
- Gas CoC keywords (compliance certificates) have very low competition — easy wins

### The Three-Layer SEO System
```
LAYER 1: Google Business Profile (Maps 3-Pack)
  → Captures "plumber near me" searches
  → Fastest to rank, highest conversion rate
  → Requires: reviews, photos, posts, categories

LAYER 2: Service Pages (Organic Rankings)
  → Captures named service searches
  → "geyser repair cape town", "blocked drain sea point"
  → Requires: one page per service, optimised content, internal links

LAYER 3: Blog & Suburb Content (Long-Tail Traffic)
  → Captures research queries and suburb-specific searches
  → Builds domain authority over time
  → Requires: consistent publishing, suburb-specific pages
```

### 6-Month SEO Goals
| Metric | Month 3 Target | Month 6 Target |
|---|---|---|
| Google Maps 3-pack appearances | "emergency plumber cape town" | 3+ primary keywords |
| Organic phone calls per month | 10–20 | 30–60 |
| Google reviews | 30+ at 4.8+ | 60+ at 4.9+ |
| Indexed pages | 20+ | 40+ |
| Average position (primary keywords) | Top 10 | Top 5 |

## 3. LOCAL SEO STRATEGY (CAPE TOWN)

### 3.1 The Maps 3-Pack is the #1 Priority

When someone searches "plumber near me" or "emergency plumber cape town" on their phone, they see the Google Maps 3-pack BEFORE organic results. This is where most emergency phone calls originate.

**Ranking Factors for Cape Town Maps 3-Pack** (in priority order):
1. **Proximity** to searcher's location (cannot be controlled — focus on other factors)
2. **Google Business Profile completeness** (every field filled in)
3. **Review quantity and velocity** (more recent reviews = better signal)
4. **Review rating** (target 4.8+; below 4.5 is very hard to rank)
5. **Keyword relevance** (business name, categories, description, posts)
6. **Website SEO authority** (GBP links to website; website strength matters)
7. **Local citations** (consistent NAP on directories)

### 3.2 NAP Consistency (Non-Negotiable)

**NAP = Name, Address, Phone**

These three must be **100% identical** everywhere online:
- Google Business Profile
- Website (every page — header, footer, schema)
- Facebook page
- All directory listings
- Email signatures
- Van branding (phone number format must match)

**Correct format**:
- Name: `Ribbon Plumbing And Gas Heating` (exact, no abbreviation)
- Phone: `+27 XX XXX XXXX` (international format) — also display as `0XX XXX XXXX` (local)
- Address: `[Street No] [Street Name], [Suburb], Cape Town, [Postal Code], Western Cape`

**Never**:
- Abbreviate "And" to "&" in different places
- Use different phone numbers across platforms (use call tracking numbers only in ads, not in directory listings)

### 3.3 Citation Building Strategy

Submit to South African directories in this order:

**Tier 1 — Essential**:
1. Google Business Profile (already covered in Section 5)
2. Yelp South Africa (yelp.co.za)
3. Snupit (snupit.co.za) — major SA trade directory
4. Bark.co.za — high-traffic SA lead platform
5. Procompare.co.za — visible in search results for Cape Town plumber queries
6. PlumberFinder (plumberfinder.co.za) — niche plumbing directory

**Tier 2 — Local SA Business Directories**:
7. Brabys (brabys.com)
8. SA Yellow Pages (yellowpages.co.za)
9. Cylex South Africa (cylex.co.za)
10. Business Directory SA (businessdirectory.co.za)
11. Gumtree (gumtree.co.za/plumbers/cape-town) — high traffic, free listing
12. Kandua (kandua.com) — vetted home service pros in SA

**Tier 3 — Industry-Specific**:
13. PIRB member directory listing (pirb.co.za) — if available
14. City of Cape Town approved plumber list (capetown.gov.za)
15. Hellopeter (hellopeter.com) — SA's main business review platform (set up profile, respond to reviews)

- Emergency: `Burst pipe? No hot water? Ribbon Plumbing arrives in 2 hours, 24/7. PIRB-registered. Fixed prices. Call +27 XX XXX XXXX now.` (125 chars)
- Gas CoC: `Need a Gas Certificate of Compliance in Cape Town? SAQCC Gas-registered team. We issue CoCs within 48 hours. Book online.` (122 chars)

**Rules**:
- Maximum 155 characters
- Include primary keyword near the beginning
- Include a clear CTA ("Call now", "Get a free quote", "Book online")
- Never duplicate meta descriptions across pages

### 7.3 H-Tag Hierarchy

```
H1 — ONE per page. Contains primary keyword. Descriptive.
  H2 — Section headings. Each covers a major topic.
    H3 — Sub-points within a section.
      H4 — FAQ items, small detail points.
```

**Rules**:
- Never skip heading levels
- H1 is set by the marketing need (exact keyword match or close variant)
- H2s should include secondary keywords naturally — don't force them
- Never use headings purely for visual styling (use CSS for that)
- H3 on cards/list items — do not overuse

### 7.4 Keyword Placement Rules

| Location | Rule |
|---|---|
| H1 | Primary keyword, exact or close match |
| First 100 words | Primary keyword appears naturally |
| H2 (first) | Include keyword variant or related term |
| Alt text (hero image) | Location + service + company |
| Meta title | Primary keyword first |
| URL slug | Primary keyword, hyphenated |
| Schema `name` field | Exact match to primary keyword for the service |

**Keyword Density**: There is no target percentage. Write for humans. Google knows what the page is about from context. Forcing keywords in every paragraph is counterproductive.

### 8.5 .htaccess (Apache)

```apache
# Force HTTPS

RewriteCond %{REQUEST_FILENAME} !-d
RewriteCond %{REQUEST_FILENAME}.php -f
RewriteRule ^([^\.]+)$ $1.php [NC,L]

# Enable GZIP compression
<IfModule mod_deflate.c>
  AddOutputFilterByType DEFLATE text/html text/css application/javascript text/plain
```

### 8.6 Mobile-First Indexing

Google uses mobile version of site for all indexing. Requirements:
- Same content on mobile as desktop (no content hidden on mobile)
- Viewport meta tag: `<meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1">`
- Font size minimum 16px on mobile
- Clickable elements minimum 44×44px touch target
- No horizontal scrolling on any mobile screen width
- Page must be fully functional without JS (progressive enhancement)

## 10. CONVERSION OPTIMIZATION STRATEGY

### 10.1 SEO Traffic Must Convert to Calls

Ranking is worthless without conversion. Every landing page must follow this formula:

```
Headline → matches search intent exactly
Subheadline → addresses the pain point
Trust signal → immediately (badge, review count, certification)
Primary CTA → phone call (orange button, above fold)
Secondary CTA → WhatsApp (for non-call-ready visitors)
Body content → confirms expertise, builds confidence
Reviews → social proof specific to this service
FAQ → handles remaining objections
Final CTA → form or phone
```

### 10.2 Mobile SEO = Mobile Calls

60%+ of emergency plumbing searches happen on mobile. Mobile SEO = mobile conversion:

- Phone number appears in `<title>` meta for click-to-call preview (optional but effective for emergency page)
- Sticky bottom bar: Call + WhatsApp — always visible
- Page loads in under 2.5 seconds on 3G (South African mobile network reality)
- No pop-ups that block the screen on mobile (penalised by Google since 2017)
- Large, thumb-friendly buttons

### 10.3 Emergency Page Conversion Priority

The `/emergency-plumber-cape-town` page is the highest-converting page on the site. It must:

1. Load in under 2 seconds
2. Show the phone number within 2 seconds of page load (no long hero animations)
3. Have a "Call Now" button that is the FIRST interactive element in the DOM after the logo
4. Include an emergency short form (name + phone + "what's the problem" — 3 fields only)
5. State 24/7 availability explicitly in the H1 or subheadline
6. Show current availability signal: "Available Now" or "Responding Today" badge
7. Include suburb names in the copy — "Serving Sea Point, Claremont, Table View and all Cape Town suburbs 24/7"

### 10.4 Suburb Page Conversion

Each suburb page must feel locally specific — not a templated clone with the suburb name swapped:

- Include a reference to the suburb's character or housing type ("Cape Town's Southern Suburbs are home to many pre-1960s homes with aging galvanised pipes...")
- Reference local landmarks or cross-streets where applicable
- Use the suburb name multiple times naturally throughout copy
- Unique H1, unique meta description, unique opening paragraph
- Shared: service list, trust signals, CTA, form
