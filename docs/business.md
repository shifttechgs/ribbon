## 3. SERVICES ANALYSIS

### Service Line A: Plumbing Services

---

#### 3A.1 Emergency Plumbing

**Description**: Rapid-response plumbing for urgent, time-critical failures that risk property damage or loss of water supply.

**Customer Pain Points**:
- Burst pipes flooding the house at midnight or during load shedding
- Water pouring under the door and no idea who to call
- Every minute of delay means more water damage to floors and walls
- Fear of being overcharged in an emergency (knows they're desperate)
- Terrified of unregistered operators doing shoddy work with no comeback

**Emergency Level**: CRITICAL — 0–2 hour response window expected

**Typical Customer Intent Signals**:
- Searching "emergency plumber Cape Town NOW"
- Searching "plumber near me open now"
- Calling from a found number without reading the whole website
- WhatsApp message with panic: "pipe burst help"

**Objections**:
- "Are you actually available right now?"
- "How much will this cost at this hour?"
- "Will you actually show up?"

**Cross-Sell**: Pipe inspection while on-site, full system check, geyser assessment
**Upsell**: Pipe replacement vs. repair, preventative maintenance plan

**Pricing Context** (Cape Town 2025):
- Call-out fee: R500–R1,000
- Emergency hourly rate: R600–R1,300/hr (1.5–2× standard)
- After-hours/weekend surcharge: Standard

---

#### 3A.2 Burst Pipe Repair

**Description**: Repair or replacement of damaged, corroded, cracked, or burst water supply or drain pipes.

**Customer Pain Points**:
- Water damage to ceilings, walls, and floors adds up to R10,000s in secondary damage
- Older Cape Town homes (Edwardian, Victorian) have galvanized pipes that corrode internally
- Tenants calling at inconvenient hours

**Emergency Level**: HIGH — same day or next day

**Typical Intent**: "burst pipe repair Cape Town", "pipe leaking inside wall"

**Cross-Sell**: Full pipe condition assessment, leak detection scan
**Upsell**: Full repipe of galvanized system with CPVC or copper

---

#### 3A.3 Leak Detection

**Description**: Locating hidden water leaks in walls, floors, underground pipes, and pool/spa systems using pressure testing and acoustic detection technology.

**Customer Pain Points**:
- Water bill suddenly doubled — no visible leak
- Wet patch on a wall or ceiling that keeps coming back
- Fear of major excavation to find a leak (the contractor will destroy my garden)
- Previous plumbers "had a look" but couldn't find it

**Emergency Level**: MEDIUM — within 1–5 days acceptable

**Typical Intent**: "water leak detection Cape Town", "hidden pipe leak Cape Town", "water bill too high"

**Cross-Sell**: Repair of found leak, pipe assessment
**Upsell**: Full pipe system inspection, pressure testing of entire system

**Cape Town Specific**: Cape Town municipality sends high water usage alerts. Many homeowners first discover leaks from billing anomalies. This is a conversion opportunity — include content about "why is my water bill so high in Cape Town?"

---

#### 3A.4 Drain Cleaning & Unblocking

**Description**: High-pressure jetting and mechanical unblocking of kitchen drains, bathroom drains, main sewer lines, and stormwater drains.

**Customer Pain Points**:
- Sink/shower/bath won't drain
- Sewage smell coming from drains
- Blocked main sewer backing up into bathrooms
- Cape Town's older Victorian/Edwardian suburbs have aging clay sewer pipes that collapse or root-infiltrate

**Emergency Level**: MEDIUM–HIGH (sewage backup = HIGH)

**Typical Intent**: "blocked drain Cape Town", "drain unblocking Cape Town", "blocked toilet Cape Town"

**Cross-Sell**: CCTV drain inspection, drain lining for aging pipes
**Upsell**: Root-cutting and full drain jetting service, drain maintenance contract

---

#### 3A.5 Geyser Repair & Replacement

**Description**: Repair, service, and replacement of electric, solar, and gas geysers including pressure valves, thermostats, elements, and full unit replacement.

**Customer Pain Points**:
- No hot water (most common residential plumbing call in SA)
- Geyser burst — water pouring through ceiling
- Old 15-year-old geyser that's ticking time bomb
- Need Certificate of Compliance (CoC) for insurance or sale of property
- Confusion between electric, solar, and gas options

**Emergency Level**: HIGH (no hot water) to CRITICAL (burst geyser)

**Typical Intent**: "geyser repair Cape Town", "geyser replacement Cape Town", "no hot water Cape Town", "geyser not heating Cape Town"

**Cross-Sell**: Geyser blanket, pressure control valve, drip tray installation, timer installation
**Upsell**: Upgrade from electric to gas geyser, solar geyser installation, heat pump

**Pricing Context** (Cape Town 2025):
- Geyser repair: R800–R2,500 depending on parts
- Geyser replacement (supply + install, electric): R7,000–R10,500
- Gas geyser installation: R3,450–R9,500+ (unit + install)

### Service Line B: Gas Heating Services

> Gas work requires SAQCC Gas registration. Every gas job must result in a Certificate of Compliance (CoC). This is a legal requirement under the Pressure Equipment Regulations and the OHS Act.

#### 3B.1 Gas Geyser Installation

**Description**: Supply and installation of gas instantaneous (tankless) geysers, including gas line, flue, and pressure testing. Full CoC issued.

**Customer Pain Points**:
- Tired of cold showers during load shedding (even though load shedding improved in 2025, behaviour patterns remain)
- High electricity costs from running electric geyser
- Old electric geyser burst — considering upgrade to gas
- Wants hot water even when there's no electricity

**Emergency Level**: MEDIUM (if replacing burst geyser) to LOW (planned upgrade)

**Typical Intent**: "gas geyser installation Cape Town", "convert electric to gas geyser Cape Town"

**Cross-Sell**: Gas line installation, gas cylinder setup
**Upsell**: Whole-house gas conversion, gas hob, gas fireplace

**Regulatory Note**: All gas geyser installations require SAQCC Gas registration and Gas CoC. Emphasise this on all marketing materials as competitors (unregistered operators) cannot legally issue CoCs.

## 4. CUSTOMER PERSONAS

### Persona 1: "The Anxious Homeowner" (Sarah, 42)

**Profile**: Married homeowner in Claremont or Rondebosch. Two kids. Working professional. Owns an older Cape Town home (pre-1960). Not technically inclined.

**Trigger**: Wakes up to no hot water. Or discovers wet patch on ceiling near geyser room.

**Pain Points**:
- Has no idea who to call — doesn't have a go-to plumber
- Terrified of being ripped off or told the problem is worse than it is
- Cannot take time off work to wait for a plumber all day
- Has had bad experiences: plumber who didn't show up, or gave surprise invoice

**Desired Outcome**:
- Someone shows up quickly, tells them what's wrong in plain English, gives a clear price, fixes it neatly, and leaves the bathroom cleaner than they found it

**Buying Decision**: Emotional + trust-driven. Reads reviews carefully. Calls the one with the most reassuring website and clearest pricing.

**Best Channels**: Google search, Facebook, recommendations from neighbours (Facebook groups), Google Maps

**Conversion Trigger**: Phone call from a calm, professional voice. Or a website that says exactly what she needs to hear.

**Objection Responses**:
- "Too expensive" → "We give you a fixed price before we start. You only pay what we quote."
- "Not sure who to trust" → "We're PIRB-registered plumbers with 120+ 5-star Google reviews."
- "Will you actually show up?" → "We call you 20 minutes before arrival. We arrive in a 2-hour window."

---

### Persona 2: "The Property Manager" (Brendan, 38)

**Profile**: Property manager or body corporate trustee managing 5–30 residential units in Cape Town. Cape Town Northern Suburbs or Southern Suburbs.

**Trigger**: Tenant reports burst geyser at 7am on a Monday. Needs it fixed before 10am or gets calls all day.

**Pain Points**:
- Needs a plumber who is INSURED — body corporate won't accept uninsured contractors
- Needs documentation (invoice, CoC, photos) for body corporate records
- Managing multiple contractors is exhausting — wants ONE reliable plumber relationship
- Tenants complaining is a reputation risk

**Desired Outcome**:
- Fast, professional, documented. Fixed on first visit. Invoice ready for body corporate records.

**Buying Decision**: Rational + relationship-driven. Once he finds a good plumber, he stays loyal for years.

**Best Channels**: Direct referral, Google, LinkedIn (for commercial operators)

**Conversion Trigger**: Professional communication, clear invoice system, quick response to WhatsApp

**Upsell Opportunity**: Maintenance contract — guaranteed response time, priority scheduling, fixed monthly fee

---

### Persona 3: "The Airbnb Host" (Nadia, 35)

**Profile**: Owns 1–3 Airbnb properties in Atlantic Seaboard or City Bowl (Sea Point, Green Point, Camps Bay). Very income-dependent on continuous bookings.

**Trigger**: Guest checks in at 3pm, messages at 5pm that there's no hot water or a blocked drain. Review at risk.

**Pain Points**:
- A plumbing problem = lost booking income AND a bad review
- Needs after-hours response
- Needs the plumber to deal with the guest professionally (she may not be on-site)
- Needs photo evidence for their own records
- Cannot have a half-finished job left for next guest

**Desired Outcome**: Same-day fix, friendly to guests, photo of completed job sent via WhatsApp.

**Best Channels**: Facebook, Instagram, Google Maps, Cape Town Airbnb host Facebook groups

**Conversion Trigger**: "Same-day service, guest-friendly team, after-hours available." WhatsApp preferred.

**Upsell**: Regular maintenance contract — monthly or quarterly check of geysers, drains, and gas systems

---

### Persona 4: "The Emergency Caller" (Themba, 52)

**Profile**: Homeowner in any suburb. Not tech-savvy. First thought when disaster happens is to call someone. May find the number from Google Maps, a sticker on a geyser, or a neighbour's recommendation.

**Trigger**: Pipe burst. Water everywhere. NOW.

**Pain Points**:
- No time to read websites
- Needs someone on the phone RIGHT NOW
- Fears the cost but can't stop the water

**Desired Outcome**: Someone answers. Someone comes. Problem fixed.

**Buying Decision**: 100% emotional. Whoever answers the phone first gets the job.

**Best Channels**: Google search, Google Maps ("plumber near me"), phone call direct

**Conversion Trigger**: **Answering the phone.** This is the conversion. If you don't answer, they call the next number.

**Key Insight**: Emergency customers have very low price sensitivity in the moment. They pay almost anything to make the crisis stop. But if you overcharge without warning, they write a bad review. Manage expectations on the phone BEFORE the job starts.

### What We Own
- **The "one team, everything" position**: Competitors either do plumbing OR gas, rarely both with proper certification.
- **The "compliance-first" position**: Every job has paperwork. PIRB + SAQCC + CoC on every relevant job.
- **The "honest price" position**: Quote before starting. Fixed price. No surprises.

### What We Avoid
- Competing on being the cheapest (race to the bottom; attracts price-sensitive customers who leave bad reviews)
- Promising "30 minute response" (sets up failure; rather promise 2-hour window and beat it)
- Being vague about certifications (say the registration numbers out loud)

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Emergency call | Calm, reassuring, action-oriented. "We're on our way." |
| Quote / planning | Professional, clear, educational. "Here are your options." |
| After job completion | Warm, confident, relationship-building. "Any questions, just WhatsApp us." |

### Language Rules
- Say **"geyser"** not "water heater" or "boiler"
- Say **"blocked drain"** not "clogged pipe"
- Say **"gas CoC"** not "certificate" only — give it its full name first
- Say **"fixed price"** not "flat rate" — more natural South African English
- Use **R** not "ZAR" in all pricing (South African convention)
- **Afrikaans touchpoints**: Many Cape Town customers are Afrikaans-speaking. Consider bilingual SMS confirmations ("Ons is op pad" / "We're on our way") and bilingual social media posts.

### Follow-Up Process

| Trigger | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| New lead received | Respond by call or WhatsApp | Within 5 minutes |
| Quote sent | Follow up if no response | 24 hours |
| Job completed | Send invoice + review request | Same day |
| Review not left | Send second review request | 7 days |
| Job completed | Upsell / maintenance offer | 30 days |
| No activity from customer | Re-engagement message | 6 months |

### Quote Process
1. Diagnose on-site (or get information by phone for basic services)
2. Build quote on phone/tablet (use Jobber or a simple template)
3. Send quote via WhatsApp AND email within 15 minutes of diagnosis
4. Quote must include: itemised labour, parts, travel, CoC fee (if applicable), total, validity (7 days)
5. Quote footer: PIRB number, SAQCC number, 12-month guarantee statement

### Job Booking Workflow
1. Customer approves quote (WhatsApp "OK" counts as approval)
2. Confirm booking date/time by WhatsApp
3. Send reminder day before: "See you tomorrow between [time window]. Call [number] if you need to reschedule."
4. Call 20 minutes before arrival
5. On arrival: introduce self, confirm scope, confirm price

### Customer Retention Workflow
- **Post-job**: Thank you + review request
- **Month 1**: Check-in WhatsApp: "Everything still working well?"
- **Month 6**: Seasonal maintenance reminder (pre-winter for geyser check; pre-summer for AC check if applicable)

## 14. GROWTH OPPORTUNITIES

### Immediate Revenue Opportunities (0–3 months)
1. **Facebook suburb groups**: Join and participate in Rondebosch, Claremont, Sea Point, Table View local groups — these are where homeowners ask "does anyone know a good plumber?" This is the highest-ROI activity for a new business.
2. **Google Business Profile**: Set up and fully optimise with photos, services, FAQs, and weekly posts. This is the foundation of local SEO.
3. **WhatsApp Business**: Set up with automated greeting, away message, quick replies for common questions.
4. **Van/vehicle branding**: The bakkie is a moving billboard in Cape Town. A clean wrap with the name, phone number, and "PIRB + SAQCC Registered" is worth thousands in brand impressions.

### Short-Term Growth (3–6 months)
1. **Airbnb host network**: Cape Town has one of SA's largest Airbnb markets. Build relationships with Airbnb hosts who need fast, professional contractors. One host may send 10+ jobs per year.
2. **Estate agent partnerships**: Estate agents need gas CoCs and plumbing inspections for property transfers. One estate agent referral relationship = reliable pipeline of pre-qualified leads.
3. **Body corporate / managing agent network**: One managing agent managing 10 complexes = steady work. Requires invoice system, insurance certificate on file, professional response.
4. **Gas appliance supplier partnerships**: Partner with a gas appliance showroom — they refer installation jobs, you install and invoice.

### Medium-Term Growth (6–12 months)
1. **Maintenance contracts**: Recurring monthly income. Target: Airbnb hosts, property managers, small businesses. Offer priority response, quarterly inspections, fixed monthly fee.
2. **Blog content + suburb SEO**: Start publishing suburb-specific content. "Plumber in Rondebosch" articles + suburb landing pages will capture long-tail searches with lower competition.
3. **Review velocity campaign**: Actively target 100 Google reviews. This is the primary ranking signal for local plumbing searches.
4. **Referral programme**: Formal referral incentive for past customers (R100 credit or free service for successful referrals).

### Long-Term Growth (12+ months)
1. **Second technician/vehicle**: When job volume is consistent, the highest-leverage growth action is adding a second team. Doubles revenue capacity without proportionally doubling costs.
2. **Gas appliance retail**: Supply gas geysers, hobs, and appliances as a product line in addition to installation labour. Higher margins per job.
3. **Solar geyser installations**: Growing demand. Requires additional certification (solar thermal). Premium-priced service.
4. **Neighbouring cities**: Stellenbosch, Paarl, George — expand service area as team grows.
